# Napoleon - Russian campaign

### [INTRO: LOOKING AROUND]

**Through the frost and bitter dawn,**  
**Grande Armée marches on,**  
**A land so vast, horizon fades,**  
**The scent of ash, the freezing blades.**

---

### [VERSE 1: LOOKING DOWN]

**June arrived with a ruler’s pride,**  
**“To Moscow’s gates, my foes collide!”**  
**The Neman crossed, steel forged anew,**  
**A million strong—unstoppable crew.**

**Smolensk burns, the fields retreat,**  
**Scorched earth against our pounding feet.**  
**Borodino, the blood-soaked plain,**  
**A win that only death could claim.**

**Through Moscow’s walls, the dream was near,**  
**But silence spoke what hearts should fear.**  
**Flames tore through its empty veins,**  
**Victory drowned in empty gains.**

---

### [CHORUS: THE TURN]

**What happens when ambition’s blind?**  
**Are we Napoleon, trapped in time?**  
**Fire and snow, the lessons fade,**  
**A frozen march, the price we’ve paid.**

---

### [VERSE 2: LOOKING SIDEWAYS]

**October calls, the biting air,**  
**Retreating hope, despair laid bare.**  
**Berezina’s cursed, the river screams,**  
**Dreams undone by winter’s schemes.**

**Men left broken, cannons freeze,**  
**A thousand ghosts on trembling knees.**  
**Hunger haunts, the march runs thin,**  
**Nature smiles, it always wins.**

**What drives a man to claim the stars?**  
**Is glory worth the battle scars?**  
**In Moscow’s flame or icy breath,**  
**You wonder: did it lead to death?**

### [BRIDGE: THE REALIZATION]

**No crown withstands the coldest truth,**  
**Dreams burn fast, like Moscow’s roof.**  
**One man’s will may chart the skies,**  
**But winter breaks the strongest ties.**

**Now time will write upon the ash,**  
**That vision fails if hearts don’t last.**

---

### [FINAL CHORUS: LOOKING UP]

**Oh stars, what calls to men so high?**  
**The frost will answer, swords defy.**  
**We march for glory, blind to cost—**  
**The Russian snow reminds what’s lost.**

---

### [OUTRO: THE LANDING]

**Through wreckage rise the echoes still,**  
**Napoleon’s fate echoes a will.**  
**A lesson left in frozen skies—**  
**To rule too far will see one’s rise despised.**
